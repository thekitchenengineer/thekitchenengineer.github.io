The Science of Copper Pans

Why chefs love copper pans — exploring heat conductivity and material science.

Copper cookware has been cherished for centuries, not just for its beauty but also for its remarkable ability to conduct heat. But what does that mean in practice?

Ad space

The Science of Thermal Conductivity

Thermal conductivity refers to how quickly and evenly a material transfers heat. Copper ranks among the highest in thermal conductivity, far surpassing stainless steel and aluminum. This means that when you place a copper pan on heat, it warms quickly and distributes heat evenly across the surface.

Why This Matters in Cooking

Even heating reduces “hot spots” — those areas of a pan that burn food while the rest stays undercooked. Professional chefs value copper because it gives precise control, especially for delicate sauces or sugar work.

The Trade-Offs

Copper is reactive, meaning it can interact with acidic foods. That’s why most modern copper pans are lined with tin or stainless steel. They require care but reward you with unmatched cooking performance.
